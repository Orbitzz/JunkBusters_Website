Pricing & Estimates

How much does junk removal cost?

Most jobs run $75–$600+. Pricing is volume-based — you only pay for the space your load takes in our truck. Factors: total volume, weight (heavy C&D debris costs more due to rising landfill tipping fees across Middle TN), item type (mattresses, hot tubs, and tires carry disposal surcharges), and access difficulty. We always quote upfront before we lift a finger.

What are the disposal surcharges I should know about?

Some items carry per-item disposal fees set by the county landfill — not by us. We pass these through at cost:

  • Mattresses: $10–$20 each depending on county
  • Box springs: Charged separately at facilities that distinguish them (including Sumner County post-fire and Williamson County)
  • Shingles: Up to $100/ton at some facilities (Wilson County)
  • Freon appliances: Must be degassed by a certified tech before disposal — we'll advise
  • Tires: Charged separately at most facilities
How are estimates given?

Free on-site estimates for larger jobs — price is confirmed before any work begins. For smaller, straightforward jobs, we can give a ballpark over the phone or via photo. You're never locked into anything until you approve the quote.

What payment methods do you accept?

Cash, check, and all major credit/debit cards. Payment is collected on-site after the job is complete.

What We Haul — and What We Don't

What items do you haul?

We haul almost anything, including:

  • Furniture (sofas, beds, dressers, tables)
  • Appliances (refrigerators, washers, dryers — freon must be removed first)
  • Electronics, TVs, computers
  • Mattresses and box springs (disposal fees apply)
  • Yard waste and brush
  • Construction and renovation debris
  • Hot tubs (disassembled on-site)
  • Sheds (demolished on-site)
  • Garage, estate, and eviction contents
  • Bulk cardboard
  • Scrap metal — we also buy copper and aluminum at competitive rates
What items do you NOT haul?

We don't haul hazardous materials. These items require county hazardous waste drop-off events (most counties hold them quarterly):

  • Wet or liquid paint
  • Car batteries
  • Propane or oxygen tanks
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Biohazard or medical waste
  • Freon appliances without a degassing certificate
  • Motor oil, pesticides, herbicides, pool chemicals

Call us if you're unsure about a specific item — we'll give you a straight answer.

Do you donate or recycle?

Yes. We sort loads and divert usable items to local donation centers and recyclers when possible. We also buy scrap copper and aluminum at competitive rates — ask about this when you call.

The Nashville Waste Crisis & Local Tipping Fees

This section explains why disposal costs have risen across Middle Tennessee — and why we're transparent about every fee on your estimate.

Nashville Waste Crisis — Southern Services Landfill (2022–present)

Waste Management's Southern Services C&D landfill in Bordeaux — the only commercial construction and demolition landfill in Davidson County — quietly stopped accepting third-party haulers around 2022 to preserve remaining capacity for WM's own fleet. In September 2023, the Tennessee Court of Appeals denied their expansion permit. Without expansion and without third-party access, every independent junk hauler in Nashville must drive to Williamson, Sumner, Rutherford, Wilson, or Cheatham County — adding both transport time and higher tipping fees. WM has announced a planned recycling transfer facility, but it doesn't solve C&D disposal for commercial haulers in the near term.

Sumner County Resource Authority Fire — April 2024

On April 14, 2024, a hydraulic hose burst caused a fire in the tipping area at the Resource Authority of Sumner County. The facility closed for repairs. Since reopening, Sumner County now charges separately for both mattresses AND box springs — not just mattresses. Current rates: $75/ton in-county, $85/ton out-of-county. This facility serves Gallatin, Hendersonville, Portland, and all of Sumner County.

Middle Point Landfill Capacity Crisis — Rutherford County

Middle Point Landfill on Landfill Road (Rutherford County) serves 34 counties across Middle Tennessee. It is projected to reach capacity within 4–6 years. The Rutherford County Commission has proposed capping the amount of out-of-county waste (particularly from Davidson County) it accepts to extend the landfill's life. Current gate rate: $99/ton minimum — we maintain a commercial account with volume-based rate reductions. When Middle Point closes or limits intake, haulers across the region will have fewer disposal options and prices will rise further.

Williamson County Mattress & Box Spring Policy — April 2024

Williamson County Landfill began charging $20 per mattress and $20 per box spring as of April 1, 2024. The county also changed its cardboard recycling program in August 2025 to cardboard-only drop-offs. At $75/ton, Williamson County's general tipping rate is the highest in our primary Tennessee service area. We factor this into every estimate for Franklin, Brentwood, Spring Hill, and Nolensville jobs.

Tarping Law

Tennessee law requires all loads to be covered. Uncovered load fines range $20–$50 per violation. We tarp every load — it's the professional standard and we never skip it.

Prohibited at All Facilities

Wet paint, car batteries, propane/oxygen tanks, asbestos, biohazard/medical waste, freon appliances (without degassing cert), motor oil, pool chemicals, pesticides. Most counties hold quarterly hazardous waste drop-off events for residents.
